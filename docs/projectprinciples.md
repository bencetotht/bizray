## Product Backlog Summary

### 1. Structure and Ownership

- Maintain a **prioritized list** of all features, enhancements, bug fixes, and
  technical tasks.
- The **Product Owner** is responsible for the backlog’s content,
  prioritization, and readiness.
- The backlog must be **visible and accessible** to all team members and the
  lecturer.

### 2. Content Quality

- Each item must be **clear and comprehensible**, with defined **acceptance
  criteria**.
- Items should be **traceable** to user needs, business requirements, or
  strategic goals.
- Dependencies between items must be **identified and resolved** early.

### 3. Prioritization and Readiness

- Items are **ordered by business value, urgency, risk**, or **strategic
  importance**.
- Top items must be **ready for development**: well-defined, feasible, and
  testable.
- Higher-priority items should be **broken into smaller, actionable tasks**,
  while lower-priority items can remain more general.

### 4. Continuous Refinement

- Conduct **regular backlog refinement sessions** to incorporate new feedback
  and direction changes.
- Keep the backlog **updated and relevant** throughout the project.
- Ensure **transparency**: everyone understands what’s next and why.

### 5. Collaboration and Communication

- Team members must **coordinate regularly**, keeping the Scrum board updated.
- Use the **team’s MS Teams channel** as the primary space for collaboration and
  status updates.
- Maintain **open communication** and **shared visibility** of priorities and
  progress.

### 6. Agile Working Principles

- Teams are **empowered and self-organizing**, making collective decisions.
- Maintain **simplicity** and **adaptability**—plans evolve with feedback.
- Focus on **continuous improvement** via retrospectives and refinements.

### 7. Sprint Review Expectations

- Present the **Sprint Goal**, completed and unfinished items, and **demonstrate
  working product features**.
- Provide a **brief product backlog outlook** at the end of each sprint.
- Ensure **whole team participation** in each review (Product Owner, Scrum
  Master, Developers).

### 8. Deliverables per Sprint

- Upload a **snapshot** of the current **Product Backlog** and **Sprint
  Backlog** to MS Teams.
- The **Scrum Master** is responsible for submission at the end of each sprint.
- Be ready to **present** the results during the sprint review session.
